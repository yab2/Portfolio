Printer Troubleshooting Guide

A step-by-step guide for resolving common printer issues on Windows and Mac

Printers can stop working for many reasons. The problem might be with your computer, the printer itself, or the connection between them. This guide walks you through the most effective fixes to get your printer working again.

1. Check the Basics First

Before diving into software fixes, let's rule out the simple stuff. Many printer problems come from loose cables, empty ink, or low paper.

Here's what to check:

  • Paper: Make sure there's enough paper in the tray and it's loaded correctly.
  • Ink or toner: Check if the ink levels are low or empty.
  • Cables: For wired printers, make sure the USB cable is firmly connected at both ends.
  • Wi-Fi: For wireless printers, confirm the printer and computer are on the same network.

Pro Tip: Low-quality paper can cause "ghost paper jams" that stop your printer from working even when there's no visible jam.

2. Run the Windows Printer Troubleshooter

Windows has a built-in tool that can find and fix many printer problems automatically. It's a great first step before trying more complex solutions.

How to run it on Windows 11:

  1. Open Settings → Bluetooth & devices → Printers & scanners
  1. Click on your printer from the list.

Note: If your printer shows as "Offline," you'll need to fix the connection issue first before continuing.

  1. Click Run the troubleshooter and wait for it to scan and fix any issues.

Alternative way to access the troubleshooter:

Go to Settings → System → Troubleshoot → Other troubleshooters and click Run next to "Printer."

On Windows 10:

Go to Settings → Update & Security → Troubleshoot → Additional troubleshooters → Printer → Click Run the troubleshooter

3. Set Your Printer as the Default

Sometimes your computer sends print jobs to the wrong printer. Windows can automatically change the default printer based on your location, which isn't always helpful. Let's make sure the right printer is selected.

On Windows:

  1. Go to Settings → Bluetooth & devices → Printers & scanners
  1. Click on the printer you want to use
  1. Click the Set as default button

On Mac:

  1. Go to System Preferences → Printers & Scanners
  1. Right-click on your printer and select Set default printer

4. Clear the Print Queue

When you print something, your computer creates a "print job" and puts it in a queue. Sometimes these jobs get stuck and block everything behind them. Clearing the queue often fixes this.

Clear the Queue on Windows:

  1. Go to Settings → Bluetooth & devices → Printers & scanners and select your printer
  1. Click Open print queue
  1. In the menu bar, click Printer → Cancel All Documents
  1. Click Yes to confirm

After clearing the queue, try printing a test page to see if it worked:

Clear the Queue on Mac:

  1. Go to System Preferences → Printers & scanners
  1. Select your printer and click Open Print Queue
  1. Click the X icon next to each stuck job to remove it

5. Update or Reinstall Printer Drivers

Drivers are software that help your computer talk to your printer. If they're outdated or corrupted, your printer won't work properly. Here's how to update them.

Update the Driver:

  1. Right click the Start Menu (or press Windows + X) and select Device Manager
  1. Expand the Printers section
  1. Right-click your printer and select Update driver
  1. Choose Search automatically for drivers

Pro Tip: You can also download the latest driver directly from your printer manufacturer's website (HP, Canon, Brother, Epson, etc.) for the most up-to-date version.

If updating doesn't work, try reinstalling:

  1. In Device Manager, right-click your printer and select Uninstall device
  1. Restart your computer
  1. Windows will automatically reinstall the driver, or you can install one you downloaded from the manufacturer

6. Restart the Print Spooler Service

The Print Spooler is a Windows service that manages all print jobs. If it glitches, your printer stops responding. Restarting it often fixes the problem.

How to restart it:

  1. Press Windows + R to open the Run dialog
  1. Type services.msc and press Enter
  1. Scroll down and find Print Spooler
  1. Right-click it and select Restart

Pro Tip: If the service keeps stopping, there might be a deeper issue with your Windows installation. Consider running a system file check by opening Command Prompt as administrator and typing: sfc /scannow

Still Having Problems?

If none of these steps work, here are a few more things to try:

  • Reboot everything: Turn off your printer and computer. Wait 30 seconds, then turn them back on.
  • Check for Windows updates: Sometimes printer fixes come through system updates.
  • Look at your printer's display: Error codes or blinking lights can tell you exactly what's wrong. Check your printer's manual for what they mean.
  • Use the manufacturer's software: HP, Canon, and other brands have their own diagnostic tools that can help.
